## I. Executive Summary

Portola Valley, a small, affluent San Mateo County town of ~4,400, is in strong fiscal health: it carries roughly $19.5 million in total fund reserves against ~$11.9 million in annual spending, pre-funds its pension and retiree-medical obligations, and shows no bonded debt. The main watch item in the FY2024-25 budget is a planned drawdown of unassigned general-fund balance — from $2.30M to $372K — largely to fund a one-time capital project.

**Total Budget:** $11,865,024 all funds (FY2024-25); general fund $8,683,381 &nbsp;|&nbsp; **Per Capita:** ~$1,953 general fund / ~$2,668 all funds (pop. 4,446) &nbsp;|&nbsp; **Fund Balance:** General fund projected ending balance $2,535,143 (~29% of GF spending, above the 15% norm); total town reserves ~$19.48M. Unassigned GF balance is planned to fall to $372,426 after a ~$2.9M appropriated drawdown. &nbsp;|&nbsp; **Debt Load:** No bonded debt or debt-service fund appears in the FY2024-25 budget; the town contracts police, fire and dispatch services

Portola Valley reports no bonded debt or debt-service fund in the FY2024-25 budget. Rather than carrying long-term debt, it pre-funds long-term obligations through assigned reserves for pension ($712,846) and OPEB ($834,871), and funds capital projects from accumulated reserves.

Strong transparency: the full budget is published on the OpenGov platform, the town uses a formal balanced-budget model, maintains a 60% general-fund reserve policy (well above the GFOA 8-17% norm) given disaster risk, and assigns reserves for pension, OPEB, equipment, capital and legal contingencies.

## VIII. Policy Options & Strategic Recommendations

1) Adopt a multi-year plan to replenish the unassigned general-fund balance toward the town’s 60% target after the FY2024-25 capital drawdown. 2) Continue pre-funding pension and OPEB via assigned reserves. 3) Maintain disaster-resilience capital investment given landslide/wildfire exposure. 4) Explore modest revenue diversification given heavy reliance on property tax. 5) Sustain the OpenGov transparency practices.
